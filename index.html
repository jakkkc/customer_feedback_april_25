Guest Satisfaction Analysis

January - April 2025 Performance Review

Project Overview

This comprehensive analysis examines guest satisfaction across all aspects of Hunters Paradise Cottages from January to April 2025. Using data from over 15 different service categories, we've identified key strengths, improvement opportunities, and emerging trends to understand our guest experience landscape. Overall Satisfaction Trends

Monthly breakdown of all satisfaction levels (Excellent to Poor) across all service categories

What This Shows

This bar chart shows how guests rated their experience each month, categorized into five levels: Excellent, Very Good, Good, Average, and Poor.

  • January: Most guests rated their stay as Excellent (about 60%), with Very Good and Good following at around 20% and 10%, respectively. Only a small number gave Average or Poor rating
  • February: There was a big jump in Excellent ratings, rising to about 80%, while Very Good decreased to 10% and Good slightly dropped. Average and Poor remained very low.
  • March & April: The trend stayed strong with Excellent ratings around 70%, while Very Good climbed slightly. Good, Average, and Poor remained consistently low.

What it Means

  • Consistent high satisfaction: The majority of guests are very happy with their stay.
  • February’s spike: Something must have improved or changed to create a surge in top ratings.
  • Minor fluctuations: While there are some shifts, the overall satisfaction remains overwhelmingly positive.

Service Aspect Correlations

Relationships between different service categories' Excellent ratings

What This Shows

The correlation heatmap reveals fascinating relationships between service aspects. Restaurant Service Quality (0.87) and Activity Coordination (0.83) show the strongest correlations with Staff Friendliness, suggesting guests perceive these services as interconnected experiences.

Interestingly, Wi-Fi satisfaction shows moderate correlation (0.65) with Overall Value perception, indicating that improved connectivity may positively influence guests' sense of getting good value. Bed Comfort and Bathroom Cleanliness, while scoring highest, show relatively low correlations with other services (0.2-0.4 range), suggesting they're evaluated more independently by guests.

Key Insights

Consistent Strengths

Bed Comfort and Bathroom Cleanliness have maintained perfect or near-perfect scores throughout the period. These physical aspects of the guest experience appear to be reliably excellent, with Bed Comfort achieving 100% Excellent ratings in January and February before receiving a single 'Very Good' rating in March and April. This consistency suggests our housekeeping and facilities maintenance protocols are exceptionally effective.

Most Improved

Wi-Fi Connectivity has undergone a remarkable transformation. From being our lowest-rated service in January (6.7% Excellent) to achieving 33.3% Excellent by April, the improvement curve is one of the steepest we've observed. The timing aligns perfectly with our March infrastructure upgrades, showing immediate positive impact that continued to grow in the following month. Negative ratings disappeared completely by April, suggesting we've addressed the most severe connectivity issues.

Attention Needed

Pricing satisfaction, while improving from 25% Excellent in January to 62.5% in April, remains our lowest-scoring category. The data shows guests are responding positively to our value-added packages introduced in February, but there's still a perception gap compared to other aspects of the experience. Interestingly, pricing satisfaction shows stronger correlation with intangible services (like staff interactions) than with physical amenities.

Strong Correlations

The 0.87 correlation between Restaurant Service Quality and Staff Friendliness is among the strongest relationships in our data. This suggests guests don't silo their perceptions of service quality by department, but rather form holistic impressions that carry across different interactions. Similarly, the 0.83 correlation between Activity Coordination and Friendliness indicates that well-organized activities enhance perceptions of staff warmth and approachability.
