Guest Satisfaction Analysis

January - April 2025 Performance Review

Project Overview

This comprehensive analysis examines guest satisfaction across all aspects of Hunters Paradise Cottages from January to April 2025. Using data from over 15 different service categories, we've identified key strengths, improvement opportunities, and emerging trends to understand our guest experience landscape.

Overall Satisfaction Trends

Monthly breakdown of all satisfaction levels (Excellent to Poor) across all service categories

What This Shows

The line chart reveals a steady upward trajectory in 'Excellent' ratings across most categories since January, with particularly strong gains in March. 'Very Good' ratings have remained consistently high, forming the bulk of our satisfaction metrics. Interestingly, 'Poor' ratings have nearly disappeared by April, showing just 2 instances across all categories compared to 12 in January.

The most notable movement appears in February, where we see a temporary dip in 'Good' ratings coinciding with a spike in 'Very Good' - suggesting guests were clustering at the higher end of the satisfaction scale during this period.

Wi-Fi Connectivity Deep Dive

Detailed tracking of our most frequently mentioned improvement area

What This Shows

Wi-Fi satisfaction tells a dramatic improvement story. In January, only 6.7% of guests rated it 'Excellent', with 40% giving 'Average' or below ratings. March shows a pivotal change - the month we implemented new routers and boosters - with 'Excellent' jumping to 20% and 'Average' ratings halving.

By April, the transformation is clear: 'Excellent' ratings reached 33.3%, while negative ratings ('Poor' and 'Fair') completely disappeared. The data shows Wi-Fi is transitioning from a pain point to a strength, though still below our property-wide excellence standards.

Staff Friendliness Excellence

Percentage of "Excellent" ratings for staff friendliness by month

What This Shows

Staff friendliness maintains exceptional performance, starting strong at 80% 'Excellent' in January and climbing to 87.5% by April. The area chart shows remarkable consistency in this category, with no month dipping below 80% excellence.

What's particularly interesting is the relationship between friendliness scores and other service metrics. When we see spikes in friendliness ratings (particularly in February and April), corresponding lifts appear in restaurant service and activity coordination ratings, suggesting friendly staff positively influence perception of other services.

Service Category Rankings

All service aspects ranked by weighted satisfaction score (5=Excellent to 1=Poor)

What This Shows

Bed Comfort leads all categories with a perfect 5.0 weighted score, followed closely by Bathroom Cleanliness at 4.9. These physical comfort metrics consistently outperform other aspects of the guest experience.

At the other end, Pricing Satisfaction ranks lowest (3.8), though showing steady improvement from its January position. Wi-Fi, despite its improvements, still ranks in the bottom third of categories (4.1), indicating room for continued enhancement. The middle tier contains most of our activity and dining-related services, all scoring between 4.3-4.6.

Wi-Fi Satisfaction Forecast

3-month predictive forecast for Wi-Fi Excellent ratings

What This Shows

The forecast model predicts continued growth in Wi-Fi satisfaction if current trends persist. Based on the April trajectory, we can expect 'Excellent' ratings to reach approximately 42-45% by July, which would place Wi-Fi in our top half of service categories.

The confidence interval (shaded area) shows a range of possible outcomes, with the lower bound suggesting a floor of about 38% 'Excellent' ratings even if growth slows slightly. This projection assumes no additional infrastructure investments beyond what's already planned.

Service Aspect Correlations

Relationships between different service categories' Excellent ratings

What This Shows

The correlation heatmap reveals fascinating relationships between service aspects. Restaurant Service Quality (0.87) and Activity Coordination (0.83) show the strongest correlations with Staff Friendliness, suggesting guests perceive these services as interconnected experiences.

Interestingly, Wi-Fi satisfaction shows moderate correlation (0.65) with Overall Value perception, indicating that improved connectivity may positively influence guests' sense of getting good value. Bed Comfort and Bathroom Cleanliness, while scoring highest, show relatively low correlations with other services (0.2-0.4 range), suggesting they're evaluated more independently by guests.

Key Insights

Consistent Strengths

Bed Comfort and Bathroom Cleanliness have maintained perfect or near-perfect scores throughout the period. These physical aspects of the guest experience appear to be reliably excellent, with Bed Comfort achieving 100% Excellent ratings in January and February before receiving a single 'Very Good' rating in March and April. This consistency suggests our housekeeping and facilities maintenance protocols are exceptionally effective.

Most Improved

Wi-Fi Connectivity has undergone a remarkable transformation. From being our lowest-rated service in January (6.7% Excellent) to achieving 33.3% Excellent by April, the improvement curve is one of the steepest we've observed. The timing aligns perfectly with our March infrastructure upgrades, showing immediate positive impact that continued to grow in the following month. Negative ratings disappeared completely by April, suggesting we've addressed the most severe connectivity issues.

Attention Needed

Pricing satisfaction, while improving from 25% Excellent in January to 62.5% in April, remains our lowest-scoring category. The data shows guests are responding positively to our value-added packages introduced in February, but there's still a perception gap compared to other aspects of the experience. Interestingly, pricing satisfaction shows stronger correlation with intangible services (like staff interactions) than with physical amenities.

Strong Correlations

The 0.87 correlation between Restaurant Service Quality and Staff Friendliness is among the strongest relationships in our data. This suggests guests don't silo their perceptions of service quality by department, but rather form holistic impressions that carry across different interactions. Similarly, the 0.83 correlation between Activity Coordination and Friendliness indicates that well-organized activities enhance perceptions of staff warmth and approachability.
