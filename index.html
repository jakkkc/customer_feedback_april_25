Guest Satisfaction Analysis

January - April 2025 Performance Review

Project Overview

This comprehensive analysis examines guest satisfaction across all aspects of Hunters Paradise Cottages from January to April 2025. Using data from over 15 different service categories, we've identified key strengths, improvement opportunities, and emerging trends to understand our guest experience landscape. Overall Satisfaction Trends

Monthly breakdown of all satisfaction levels (Excellent to Poor) across all service categories

What This Shows

This bar chart shows how guests rated their experience each month, categorized into five levels: Excellent, Very Good, Good, Average, and Poor.

  • January: Most guests rated their stay as Excellent (about 60%), with Very Good and Good following at around 20% and 10%, respectively. Only a small number gave Average or Poor rating
  • February: There was a big jump in Excellent ratings, rising to about 80%, while Very Good decreased to 10% and Good slightly dropped. Average and Poor remained very low.
  • March & April: The trend stayed strong with Excellent ratings around 70%, while Very Good climbed slightly. Good, Average, and Poor remained consistently low.

What it Means

  • Consistent high satisfaction: The majority of guests are very happy with their stay.
  • February’s spike: Something must have improved or changed to create a surge in top ratings.
  • Minor fluctuations: While there are some shifts, the overall satisfaction remains overwhelmingly positive.

Key Insights

Consistent Strengths

The analysis highlights room cleanliness and staff friendliness as standout strengths, consistently receiving high ratings across all months. Guests appreciate the clean, well-maintained accommodations and warm hospitality, which are key factors in their satisfaction. These services set a positive tone for the overall guest experience and should remain a priority in branding efforts

Most Improved

Wi-Fi connectivity and general service quality saw major improvements, especially in March. Initially, February had lower ratings, suggesting connectivity issues or service inconsistencies. However, corrective measures led to higher ratings in March and April, showing successful enhancements. This upward trend indicates effective problem-solving efforts, reinforcing the importance of maintaining high standards and proactively addressing guest concerns.

Attention Needed

Food quality and entertainment received a mix of ratings, with more guests ranking them as Good rather than Excellent. While not a major weakness, this suggests an opportunity for refinement. Menu variety, presentation, and dining experience enhancements could push ratings higher. Entertainment and extra amenities could also benefit from refreshing activities or immersive experiences to make stays more engaging.

Strong Correlations

Predictive analytics and heat map analysis suggest direct links between service quality improvements and guest satisfaction trends. Higher staff friendliness ratings often aligned with increases in overall hotel ratings, showing that positive interactions strongly influence guest experiences. Additionally, seasonal shifts impacted satisfaction levels, meaning adjusting service delivery during high-demand periods could optimize guest experience.
